Sisig

Sisig is a popular Filipino dish made from chopped pork, usually pig's head and liver, seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers. The pork is traditionally grilled, then sautéed with the other ingredients until crispy. Often served sizzling hot on a cast-iron plate, sisig is a flavorful mix of savory, tangy, and spicy, perfect as a main dish or bar snack.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork belly (or pig’s head if preferred)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 Thai chili peppers, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • Calamansi or lemon, for serving

Steps

  1. Grill the Pork: Boil the pork belly until tender, then grill or broil it until the skin is crispy. Let it cool slightly, then chop it into small pieces.
  2. Sauté the Ingredients: In a hot pan, melt the butter and sauté garlic and onions until translucent.
  3. Mix the Pork: Add the chopped pork to the pan, then stir in the soy sauce, vinegar, and black pepper. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  4. Add the Chili: Mix in the chopped chili peppers and adjust seasoning with salt. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  5. Optional Mayonnaise & Egg: Stir in the mayonnaise for a creamier texture. If you prefer, crack an egg on top and mix it into the sizzling pork just before serving.
  6. Serve: Serve hot, garnished with calamansi or lemon on the side.
