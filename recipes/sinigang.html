Sinigang

Sinigang is a classic Filipino soup known for its tangy and savory flavor. Made with a choice of pork, beef, shrimp, or fish, itâ€™s simmered with a variety of vegetables like radish, eggplant, and kangkong (water spinach). The signature sour taste comes from tamarind or other souring agents like calamansi or green mango, making sinigang a comforting and refreshing dish, perfect for any meal.

Sinigang

  • 1 lb pork belly or ribs (can substitute with shrimp or fish)
  • 1 packet sinigang mix or 1 cup tamarind paste
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 2-3 pieces taro, peeled and quartered
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach) or bok choy
  • 3-4 long green chili peppers (optional)
  • 6 cups water
  • Fish sauce to taste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. Boil the Meat: In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the pork, onions, and tomatoes. Simmer until the pork is tender, about 45 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Add Vegetables: Add the taro and cook until slightly tender, about 10 minutes. Then add the radish and eggplant.
  3. Season the Soup: Pour in the sinigang mix or tamarind paste. Stir well and adjust the sourness to your taste. Season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper.
  4. Final Touches: Add the green chili peppers (if using) and kangkong or bok choy. Let it simmer for another 2-3 minutes until the greens are wilted.
  5. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice, enjoying the tangy and savory flavors.
