Menudo

Menudo is a beloved Filipino stew made with tender pork and liver, slowly simmered in a rich tomato sauce. It’s mixed with diced potatoes, carrots, and bell peppers, often including raisins and green peas for a hint of sweetness. This hearty and flavorful dish is a staple in Filipino households, perfect for everyday meals or special occasions.

Menudo

  • 1 lb pork shoulder, cubed
  • 1/2 lb pork liver, cubed
  • 2 potatoes, cubed
  • 2 carrots, cubed
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil
  1. Marinate the Pork: In a bowl, combine the pork cubes with soy sauce. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Sauté the Ingredients: In a large pan, heat oil and sauté garlic and onions until fragrant. Add the marinated pork and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Add the Tomato Sauce: Stir in the tomato paste and tomato sauce, then add the bay leaves. Simmer for about 10 minutes.
  4. Add the Vegetables: Add the potatoes and carrots. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 10-15 minutes.
  5. Incorporate the Liver: Add the pork liver and cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Final Touches: Stir in the bell peppers, raisins, green peas, sugar, salt, and pepper. Simmer for an additional 3-5 minutes until everything is well combined and cooked through.
  7. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice, enjoying the rich and hearty flavors.
